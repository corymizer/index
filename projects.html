Sixth Day Soccer

Recently I got the chance to work with David over at Sixth Day Soccer as he was creating a new website and wanted a new logo to go along with it. David had decided he wanted something that was simple and flat, but also something different from the normal type of soccer logo (podcasts, blogs) that's out there. We decided to make it less about soccer and just more about the name. The only real inclusion of soccer we used was the old school 'volleyball' style ball, and to an extent, the date. I designed this mainly using different shades of green and an off white kind of trying to keep in line with the soccer pitch. So far, Sixth Day Soccer is using a monotone black or white logo on the new website (see below), which looks great (especially the white) overlaid onto old school soccer photos on the site's main page.

Wolverhampton Wanderers FC

Wolverhampton Wanderers are a proud club based in the West Midlands of England, just outside of Birmingham, in Wolverhampton. Although recently suffering back to back relegations to League 1, the club won the English Championship this season, the 2nd division in England, and have jumped back into the Premier League. Wolves, as they are known, have won the top flight on three occasions, the last one being 1958-'59, and have lifted the FA Cup 4 times, and the League Cup twice. I choose to re-brand Wolves because of the history of the club. The logos of the glory days and into the 70's were my amoung my favorite soccer badges, and although I have zero issue with the club's current badge, the 3 wolves leaping through the air and the single wolf jumping the name will always remain favorites.
